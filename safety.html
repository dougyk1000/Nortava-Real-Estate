Safety Tips

Stay safe while searching for your new home

Important Warning

Never pay rent or deposits before viewing the property in person. Scammers may create fake listings to collect money. Always meet the landlord and verify ownership.

Before Viewing

Verify the Landlord

Look for verified badges on listings. Ask for identification when meeting. Legitimate landlords won't mind proving ownership.

Research the Area

Know the neighborhood before visiting. Ask locals about the area, check accessibility to amenities, and understand rental prices in that location.

Tell Someone

Always let a friend or family member know where you're going and when. Share the listing details and landlord contact with them.

During Viewing

Meet in Public First

Consider meeting the landlord in a public place first before going to the property. This gives you a chance to assess them.

Bring Someone Along

Never go alone to view a property, especially for evening viewings. Having a companion adds safety and a second opinion.

Check Everything

Test water taps, electricity, doors, and locks. Ask about utilities, parking, and any shared spaces. Take photos for reference.

Payments

Never Pay Before Viewing

Legitimate landlords will not ask for money before you've seen the property. Any request for advance payment is a red flag.

Get a Receipt

Always get written receipts for any payments. Keep records of all transactions and agreements.

Sign a Lease

Insist on a written lease agreement before paying rent. Read all terms carefully and keep a copy.

Red Flags

  • Price seems too good to be true
  • Landlord refuses to meet in person
  • Pressure to pay immediately or lose the property
  • Requests for unusual payment methods
  • Landlord claims to be "out of town" and can't show property
  • No proper documentation or proof of ownership
  • Vague or inconsistent answers to questions
